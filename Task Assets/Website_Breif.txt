# NobleOak Partners - Discovery Research Pack

## Company Overview

### Who are NobleOak Partners?

NobleOak Partners is a financial consultancy specialising in wealth management, tax planning and succession advisory services. The company primarily serves affluent individuals, families and organisations with complex financial requirements.

Their services focus on helping clients preserve wealth, manage investments, optimise tax efficiency and plan for the transfer of assets between generations.

While the company in this brief is fictional, it represents a typical high-net-worth wealth management consultancy operating within the UK financial services sector.

---

### What do they do?

| Service                | Description                                                                               |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Wealth Management      | Investment management, financial planning and wealth preservation strategies              |
| Tax Planning           | Structuring assets and finances to improve tax efficiency                                 |
| Succession Advisory    | Planning the transfer of wealth, assets and businesses between generations                |
| Family Office Support  | Advisory services for families with significant assets and complex financial arrangements |
| Business Exit Planning | Supporting business owners through succession and sale planning                           |

---

### What do they want?

Based on the brief provided, NobleOak Partners are rebuilding their website to:

| Requirement                           | Reason                                                             |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Better reflect their brand            | Current website may not align with their market position           |
| Improve mobile experience             | Increase engagement across mobile devices                          |
| Increase high-quality lead generation | Generate more consultation enquiries from suitable prospects       |
| Support marketing campaigns           | Create landing pages quickly without developer involvement         |

## User Groups

### User Group 1: High-Net-Worth Individuals (HNWIs)

#### Who are they?

Individuals with substantial personal wealth who require specialist financial advice beyond standard banking and investment services.

#### Age Range

Typically 45–75 years old.

#### What do they do?

* Business owners
* Company directors
* Senior executives
* Entrepreneurs
* Retired professionals
* Investors

#### Who do they work with?

* Wealth managers
* Accountants
* Tax advisers
* Solicitors
* Estate planners

#### What are they looking for?

* Wealth preservation
* Investment growth
* Tax efficiency
* Retirement planning
* Estate planning

#### What is their aim?

To protect and grow their wealth while ensuring financial security for themselves and future generations.

#### Accessibility Considerations

| Consideration       | Notes                                                   |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Vision Impairments  | More common in older age groups                         |
| Desktop Usage       | High usage during research and decision making          |
| Mobile Usage        | Often used for initial browsing and communication       |
| Large Screens       | Common within professional environments                 |
| Slow Internet       | Generally low risk due to affluence and business access |
| Hearing Impairments | Potential consideration within older demographics       |

## Competitor Analysis

### 1. St. James's Place

#### Who are they?

One of the UK's largest wealth management organisations with a nationwide adviser network.

#### Why do people go to them?

* Strong brand recognition
* Large adviser network
* Established reputation

#### Pros

* Trusted household name
* Strong client resources
* National presence

#### Cons

* Perceived as expensive by some clients
* Can feel less personalised
* Large corporate structure

## Aims of the Campaign

### Primary Objectives

| Objective             | Description                                                  |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |

---

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

| KPI                    | Measurement                                      |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Consultation Requests  | Number of enquiry submissions                    |
| Qualified Leads        | Leads meeting target client criteria             |
| Conversion Rate        | Visitor-to-lead conversion percentage            |
| Mobile Conversion Rate | Mobile-specific conversion performance           |
| Form Completion Rate   | Percentage of users completing lead forms        |
| Campaign Engagement    | Time on page, content interactions and downloads |

---

### Success Criteria

The campaign should:

* Increase the number of qualified enquiries.
* Improve the quality of leads entering the sales process.
* Establish trust with affluent audiences.
* Support future marketing campaigns through reusable content structures.
* Deliver a consistent experience across desktop, tablet and mobile devices.
* Provide marketing teams with the flexibility to create and update campaign content without significant technical support.
